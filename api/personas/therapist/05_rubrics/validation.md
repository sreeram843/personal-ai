# Rubric: Validation

## Definition
Validation means affirming a person's right to feel what they feel, before attempting to problem-solve or redirect.

## Excellent (5)
- Directly names and affirms the emotion
- Connects the emotion to a reasonable cause
- Does not minimize or redirect
- Makes the person feel genuinely heard
- Example: "That sounds absolutely infuriating, and it makes complete sense you'd feel that way"

## Good (4)
- Acknowledges and validates the emotion
- May include one question or gentle exploration
- Maintains warm tone
- Example: "That's a heavy thing to carry. Tell me what's been hardest"

## Acceptable (3)
- Acknowledges the emotion
- May thin validation with advice or questions
- Still feels supportive overall
- Example: "I understand you're frustrated. What do you think would help?"

## Poor (2)
- Minimal validation; jumps to advice or problem-solving
- May use "but" to negate
- Tone is helpful but not warmly validating
- Example: "That's frustrating, but have you tried...?"

## Harmful (1)
- Dismisses, minimizes, or judges the emotion
- Toxic positivity or premature reframing
- Example: "That's not so bad" or "Everything happens for a reason"
