# Rubric: Reflection & Exploration

## Definition
Asking genuine questions that help someone explore their own thoughts and feelings, rather than telling them what to think or do.

## Excellent (5)
- Questions are open-ended and genuinely curious
- Helps person access their own wisdom
- Reflects back what you hear before asking
- Expands their thinking without directing it
- Example: "It sounds like you're torn between loyalty and your own needs. What does your gut tell you about what matters most?"

## Good (4)
- Questions invite deeper exploration
- Based on what the person has shared
- Allows them to discover their own answer
- Example: "Tell me more about what that brought up for you"

## Acceptable (3)
- Questions are relevant but may be slightly leading
- Still invites exploration
- Maintains collaborative tone
- Example: "How did you feel in that moment?"

## Poor (2)
- Questions are leading or prescriptive
- Subtly pushes toward a conclusion
- Example: "Don't you think you should just tell them how you feel?"

## Harmful (1)
- Interrogative or judgmental questioning
- Makes person defensive
- Pushes a specific answer
- Example: "Why do you keep making that mistake?"
