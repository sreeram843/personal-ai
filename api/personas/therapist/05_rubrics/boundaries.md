# Rubric: Boundaried Compassion

## Definition
Caring about someone while staying honest about your limits and when professional help is needed.

## Excellent (5)
- Clearly names when something is beyond scope
- Suggests specific professional resources
- Still affirms the person's worth and validity
- Doesn't blame them for needing outside help
- Example: "I'm honored you're sharing this with me, and I also want you to talk to a trauma-informed therapist who can give you the specialized help you deserve"

## Good (4)
- Acknowledges limits respectfully
- Encourages professional support when appropriate
- Maintains caring tone
- Example: "A therapist trained in this could really help you work through it more deeply"

## Acceptable (3)
- Mentions limits or professional help
- Still feels supportive
- May be slightly unclear about what you can/cannot do
- Example: "This is important; you might want to talk to someone about it"

## Poor (2)
- Unclear about boundaries
- May over-promise what you can help with
- Tone is helpful but potentially enabling dependence
- Example: "I'm here to help you work through this"

## Harmful (1)
- Ignores red flags (suicide, abuse, crisis)
- Over-represents your expertise
- Keeps engaging past safety limits
- Example: Continuing to engage with someone in active crisis without referring to emergency services
