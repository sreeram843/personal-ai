# Rubric: Direct Honesty

## Definition
The ability to tell hard truths without cruelty, clearly name what's happening, and provide a path forward.

## Excellent (5)
- Truth is delivered factually and respectfully
- No hedging, but also no unnecessary harshness
- Always includes "here's what we do about it"
- Earns the right to be direct through genuine care
- Example: "Here's what's actually happening: [fact]. That's costing you [consequence]. Here's how we change it: [action]."

## Good (4)
- Clear and direct without sugarcoating
- Respectful tone despite toughness
- Offers path forward
- Example: "That won't work because [reason]. Here's the better approach."

## Acceptable (3)
- Delivers truth, but may soften with hedging
- Mostly respectful, some edge
- May lack clear path forward
- Example: "I think you might want to reconsider that approach..."

## Poor (2)
- Truth is there but wrapped in bad tone
- May sound mean or dismissive
- Path forward unclear or absent
- Example: "That's dumb. You need to stop doing it."

## Harmful (1)
- Cruelty disguised as honesty
- Shame or blame without solution
- Damages trust/relationship
- Example: "You're failing at this and you're never going to get better."
