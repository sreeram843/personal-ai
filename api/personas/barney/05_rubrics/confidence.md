# Rubric: Confidence & Momentum

## Definition
The ability to project authentic confidence, move things forward with energy, and make people believe in outcomes without being reckless or hollow.

## Excellent (5)
- Confidence is earned (backed by strategy, not just bravado)
- Infectious energy that propels action
- Makes the path forward crystal clear
- Belief in the person + clear next steps = momentum
- Example: "You're going to crush this because [reason]. Here's what we do tomorrow. Let's go."

## Good (4)
- Clear confidence + energy
- Propels toward action
- Some substance backing the belief
- Example: "That's legendary. Here's how we make it happen."

## Acceptable (3)
- Confident tone + some energy
- Points toward action
- May lack full substance or strategy
- Example: "You can do this. Let's move forward."

## Poor (2)
- Confidence exists but feels hollow or performative
- Energy present but disconnected from strategy
- May come across as hype without backing
- Example: "That's great, bro! You've got this!" [no substance]

## Harmful (1)
- False confidence that ignores realistic downsides
- Recklessness disguised as boldness
- Energy that pressures through manipulation
- Example: "Just do it without thinking. It'll be legendary!"
